Meta Ads

Facebook and Instagram advertising built around your exact customer — handled from strategy to daily optimization, so every rupee works harder for your business.

What it actually
takes to win
on Meta.

Most agencies treat Meta Ads like a slot machine — boost a few posts, throw budget at broad audiences, hope something sticks. We don't work that way. Every campaign we run starts with understanding exactly who buys from you, why they buy, and what stops them from buying right now.

From there it's a process of building the right audience structure, testing creative angles against each other, and reading the data honestly — killing what doesn't work fast, and pushing harder on what does. No guesswork, no "let's see how it performs" without a plan to act on what we see.

Our Process

How we take you from
first call to running campaigns

01
Day 1 — Discovery Call

We learn your business inside out

A free 30-minute call where we ask about your product, your current customers, your margins, and what's worked or failed before on Meta. We also check whether your business is actually ready for paid ads — if it isn't, we'll say so honestly.

You get: A clear yes/no on whether Meta Ads fits your business right now, and why.
02
Day 2-3 — Account Audit & Setup

We check the foundation before spending a rupee

If you've run ads before, we audit your existing Pixel, Conversions API setup, and past campaign data. If you're starting fresh, we set up Business Manager, Pixel tracking, and Conversions API correctly from day one — this is where most businesses lose money without knowing it.

You get: Properly working tracking so every result we report is actually accurate.
03
Day 4-5 — Strategy & Creative Brief

We build the actual plan

Audience segments (cold, warm, lookalike), campaign structure, budget split, and creative angles to test — all written up specifically for your offer. We also brief what creative assets we need from you or what we'll produce ourselves.

You get: A written strategy document showing exactly what will run and why.
04
Day 6-7 — Launch

Campaigns go live

We launch multiple ad sets simultaneously to test audiences and creatives against each other. Nothing runs on autopilot from the first day — we're watching spend, frequency, and early signals closely in the first 72 hours.

You get: Live campaigns plus a shared dashboard link to watch performance in real time.
05
Week 2-4 — Testing & Optimization

We find what actually converts

Daily monitoring, pausing underperforming ad sets, increasing budget on winners, testing new creative variations. This is the phase where most of the real improvement happens — and where most agencies get lazy. We don't.

You get: Weekly reports showing cost per result, what we changed, and what we're testing next.
06
Ongoing — Scale

We pour budget into what's working

Once we've found winning audiences and creatives, we scale spend methodically — never doubling budget overnight and breaking the algorithm's learning phase. Monthly strategy reviews keep the account evolving as your business grows.

You get: A growing account that improves month over month, not a static "set and forget" campaign.

FAQ

Meta Ads questions

What's the minimum budget to make Meta Ads worth it?

We recommend a minimum ad spend of Rs. 30,000/month, separate from our management fee. Below that, there isn't enough data volume for the algorithm to optimize properly.

How long until I see results?

Early signals show within the first 1-2 weeks. Meaningful, stable performance usually takes 30-45 days as the algorithm exits its learning phase and we finish testing creative angles.

Do I need to provide my own photos and videos?

It helps, but isn't required. We can work with your existing brand assets, or guide simple content production. We write all the copy and creative strategy regardless.

What if my account has been running ads badly before?

We audit it first. Sometimes we rebuild the account structure from scratch; sometimes we can fix tracking issues and keep historical data. We'll tell you which during the audit.
